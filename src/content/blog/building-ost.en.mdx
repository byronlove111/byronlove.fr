Back in early 2025, I co-founded [OpenSource Together](https://opensource-together.com) — a platform connecting developers, designers, and creatives on open source projects. We hit 500 signups on launch day.

Here's how we got there.

## The idea

The problem was simple: most people who want to contribute to open source don't know where to start. The projects are hard to find, the maintainers are hard to reach, and the onboarding is basically nonexistent.

We wanted to fix that.

## Shipping fast

We gave ourselves a weekend. No planning docs, no roadmap, no sprint planning. Just a Figma file, a Next.js app, and a shared Notion with three bullet points.

<Callout type="tip">
  Ship ugly first. A live ugly thing beats a perfect thing that doesn't exist yet.
</Callout>

The first version had no authentication, no profiles, and the design was rough. But it worked — you could browse projects and reach out to maintainers directly.

## What actually made it work

The launch mattered more than the product. We posted on X, in developer Discord servers, in Hacker News, and in every relevant subreddit. We replied to every comment. We DM'd people personally.

<Callout type="info">
  500 signups in 24 hours came from distribution, not from the product being perfect.
</Callout>

## What I'd do differently

Build the community before the product. The signups came from people who already trusted us — from X followers, Discord communities, people who'd seen us build in public. The product was just the thing we gave them to sign up for.

Start with the audience. Then build the thing.
