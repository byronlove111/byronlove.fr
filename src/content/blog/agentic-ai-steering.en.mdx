Almost every roadmap I see now has a checkbox titled "agentic something." Rarely does the checkbox say who is allowed to do what for how long without a human noticing. That is the whole problem.

Calling a system "agentic" usually means three things slipped into one word: **it can propose multi-step actions**, **it can use external tools**, and **it can keep looping until it believes it is finished**. Useful? Often. Automatically safe or aligned with your product? Absolutely not.

## Autonomy needs a leash and a referee

Agents are tempting because they offload planning. Someone still has to own three decisions:

1. **Intent** — what success means, expressed in measurable terms rather than vibes.
2. **Authority** — which tools, data, accounts, spend, emails, merges, deletes are in scope every run.
3. **Stop conditions** — time budget, irreversible checkpoints, escalation rules when confidence drops.

Skip any of those and what you shipped is not an agent harness; it is a script dressed as intelligence, or worse, a stochastic intern with OAuth keys.

> The hard part is not letting a model draft another step. It is deciding which steps may never execute without confirmation.

Think in terms of *policies*: explicit allowlists for tools, quotas for retries, sandboxed side effects until a validation gate passes.

## Observation beats narration

Healthy agent loops resemble control systems more than chats. Something **observes reality** (logs, linter, billing API, inbox), something **plans or edits**, something **checks** whether the loop should continue.

If verification is purely "the model said it succeeded," production will surprise you. Ground truth beats self-report: failing tests, diff size limits, anomaly alerts, human escalation queues.

<CodeBlock client:load lang="text">
Intent → Plan (bounded depth) → Act (allowlisted tools) → Verify (non-LLM checks) → Human review if fail or low confidence → Stop
</CodeBlock>

You do not need a diagram for every prototype, but teams that skip the center column ("verify") reliably ship regressions disguised as progress.

## What to tell leadership

Agents are leverage; they amplify both speed and catastrophe radius. Steering is ongoing product work: tightening policies after incidents, rewriting success criteria after user research, archiving tools nobody should touch anymore.

If your org cannot say out loud **who loses sleep when the bot goes rogue**, pause the rollout and write that down before you expose customers to one more unattended loop.
